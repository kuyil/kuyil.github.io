--- layout: barebones_white_margins title: Payment Methods --- When you buy a premium feature in our app, the purchase is directly handled by Google. Apart from Credit and Debit cards, there are other ways to buy, such as Carrier Billing, Netbanking and Gift cards. This guide will walk you through these payment methods. When you touch BUY in a premium feature listing, you'll be shown screen similar to the one below. By touching the title or price in this screen, you can make Google Play show more options:
Note: These examples are based on Shruti Carnatic Tuner's premium feature Remove Ads, but, the procedure is the same for other premium features.

Redeem

Google offers Google Play Gift Cards and Google Play Recharge codes that can be used to buy in-app products. Touch Redeem if you have one of these.

Paytm

In India, you can buy Google Play Recharge from Paytm for any denomination from ₹100 to ₹1500. These images show how to get your Google Play recharge code.
Alternatively, you can buy physical Google Play Gift Cards from snapdeal or amazon.

Redeeming

Once you have a Google Play Recharge code or Gift Card, you can add it as a payment method by touching Redeem in our app.

With that, you can proceed to BUY the feature.
Instead of Redeem, if you press Payment methods in the payment screen, you'll be presented all payment options available in your area.

Use ____ billing

If you see your mobile operator's name in this option, it means carrier billing is available to you. If you choose this option, you can purchase the in-app feature without a credit/debit card, instead, you'll be charged in your next mobile bill. Currently Idea, Airtel and Vodafone networks support this facility.

Add Netbanking

Netbanking is another way to buy in-app features without credit card. Choose your bank from the list and enter credentials to make the purchase.
