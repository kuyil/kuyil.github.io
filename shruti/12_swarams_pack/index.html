Frequenty Asked Questions

Why do I need this feature?
If any of the following descriptions sound like you, you need this feature:
  • You are a carnatic vocal student who wishes to perfect singing of your swara sthanas.
  • You are a student learning carnatic instrument (violin, veena, etc.) who wants to improve accuracy of your fingering technique to play all swarams.
  • Your instrument requires tuning to swarams other than Sa, Ma1 and Pa (e.g. tambura with one string tuned to Ni).
  • Your veena's melam (frets) needs to be checked and adjusted accordingly.
  • You are a professional musician looking for precise reference tones for all swarams.
  • You are an experienced musician or advanced student who is trying to fine-tune to authentic carnatic swara sthanas instead of Western Equal Temperament Intervals (typically seen in keyboards and harmoniums).
  • Simply you love Shruti Carnatic Tuner and you would like to support the efforts of the developers.

What is included in this 12 swarams pack?
This pack extends Shruti Carnatic Tuner's capability to work for all 12 pure Carnatic swara sthanas.

Can you be more specific about the swarams supported?
All 12 carnatic swarams viz.,
  1. Sa Shadjam,
  2. Ri1 Suddha Rishabam,
  3. Ri2 Chatushruti Rishabam or Ga1 Suddha Gandharam,
  4. Ga2 Sadharana Gandharam or Ri3 Shatshruti Rishabam,
  5. Ga3 Anthara Gandharam,
  6. Ma1 Suddha Madhyamam,
  7. Ma2 Prati Madhyamam,
  8. Pa Panchamam,
  9. Dha1 Suddha Dhaivatam,
  10. Dha2 Chatushruti Dhaivatam or Ni1 Suddha Nishadam,
  11. Ni2 Kaisika Nishadam or Dha3 Shatshruti Dhaivatam and
  12. Ni3 Kakali Nishadam.

Does Automatic Swaram Detection work for all these swarams?
Yes.

Are reference tones provided for all these swarams?
Yes, in 4 Sthayis.

Where are Ga1 and Ni1?
Ri2 is same as Ga1. Dha2 is same as Ni1.

There are only two Ga and Ni as far as I know. How come the app shows Ga3 and Ni3?
Actually there are three Ri-s, Ga-s, Dha-s and Ni-s in Carnatic music. 72 Melakartha scheme is based on all these swarams. See the list above for correct swaram names and corresponding numbering.

Aren't there 16 swarams instead of just 12?
Yes. However, some of the swarams overlap, resulting in only 12 unique swara sthanas. For example, Ri3 is same as Ga2. In other words, this feature pack supports all the swarams.

What do you mean by "pure" Carnatic Swara sthanas?
The frequency ratios of Carnatic swarams are different from Western Equal temperament (used in Keyboards and Harmoniums). Shruti Carnatic Tuner and this feature pack are implemented based on authentic frequency ratios of Carnatic music. This is what we mean when we say Pure carnatic swara sthanas.

Which ratios are used for the swarams?
These are the ratios used in the feature pack:
  1. Sa 1,
  2. Ri1 16/15,
  3. Ri2 or Ga1 9/8,
  4. Ga2 or Ri3 32/27,
  5. Ga3 5/4,
  6. Ma1 4/3,
  7. Ma2 45/32,
  8. Pa 3/2,
  9. Dha1 128/81,
  10. Dha2 or Ni1 5/3,
  11. Ni2 or Dha3 16/9 and
  12. Ni3 15/8.

Why these specific ratios?
We are aware there are alternatives to few of the above ratios in the wild. Some of those ratios figure only in gamakams. The ratios presented here are also strongly backed by theory. Concluding on these specific ratios took us months of
  • research on available literature,
  • generation of extensive sound sample library,
  • considerations for the techniques employed by instrumentalists, and
  • listening tests with professional musicians.
In conclusion, the above ratios are authentic and most suitable for contemporary carnatic music practice.
